Overview

The TurtleRescue Challenge is a fast-paced robotics competition where teams program a TurtleBot4 to explore a small indoor “disaster zone,” locate victim markers, and return safely to base with a mission report. Designed for participants with preliminary knowledge in ROS and Gazebo simulator, the challenge blends autonomous navigation, perception, and lightweight mission planning in a friendly, hands-on setting.
TurtleRescue gives students a taste of real autonomous robotics and search-and-rescue mission design, using accessible tools and a playful scenario that mirrors the spirit of real rescue scenarios in a friendly format.
During the event, each team will have 30 minutes to run their robot in the arena, navigating around obstacles, detecting AprilTags or colored objects that represent victims, and logging their positions. Points are awarded for correct detections, accurate localization, safe navigation, and successful return to base. Teams may start building their solution via Gazebo and set their local environment for running experiments. During RoME, participants will have access to the physical robot (Turtlebot 4 Lite), where they can test their code. The full competition fits within a 2-hour window, including briefings, runs, and score. The winners will be awarded during the last day of RoME presentations.

  • Goals: Teams must program a TurtleBot4 to autonomously explore a predefined arena, detect target objects (victims), and return to a base with collected information.
  • Duration: 2-hour competition session + testing sessions during RoME’26.
  • Teams: Up to 4 teams (1 TurtleBot4 per match cycle).
  • Robots: 4× TurtleBot4 (standard configuration with LiDAR + RGB camera).
  • Difficulty: Introductory to intermediate, but with clear progression toward real mission engineering.

Core Learning Objectives

Students will apply knowledge in:

  • Robotics mission engineering.
  • Mission decomposition tasks: navigation, perception, and reporting.
  • Autonomous navigation fundamentals with ROS2 and Nav2.
  • Software engineering practices.
  • Simulation in ROS environment (Gazebo/Ignition).

Objectives and Score

Each team’s robot must autonomously:

  1. Start at a defined base station
  2. Map the unknown arena (SLAM)
  3. Locate victims
  4. Record their positions
  5. Return to base before time expires
  6. Deliver a final “Mission Report”, containing:
    1. List of detected victims
    2. Their coordinates (approximate)

Victims can be:

  • AprilTag markers
  • Printed images or markers representing the victims

The score will be calculated by the number of correctly identified victims and the total area explored. Time may also be used as a tiebreaker.

Rules

Time

  • Teams must submit a simulation of the mission before the start of RoME’26 (more instructions will be provided)
  • Total match duration: 25 minutes per team.

Allowed Resources

  • TurtleBot4 Lite with default sensors.
  • ROS2 Humble or Iron.
  • Custom software brought by teams.

Prohibited

  • Teleoperation after the start signal.
  • External sensors (phones, other cameras).
  • Modifying the arena physically.
  • No physical intervention after the mission starts.

Requirements

Teams must submit:

  • ROS2 workspace.
  • Launch files.
  • Short README explaining:
    1. Mission strategy
    2. Perception pipeline
    3. Exploration algorithm
    4. Safety considerations
